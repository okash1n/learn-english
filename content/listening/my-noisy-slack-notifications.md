Can I be honest with you? My Slack was driving me crazy last month. Every few minutes, I'd get a ping. A new message, a reaction, someone tagging the whole channel. It's hard to write good code when your screen keeps flashing at you. I kept losing my focus, and it was really annoying.

The worst part was the channels I didn't even care about. You know, the random ones for lunch plans or office memes. They're fun, but I don't need a notification for every joke. Plus, some people love writing one word per message. So a single thought turns into ten pings. Don't you hate that?

So one afternoon, I finally decided to fix it. I went through my channels one by one. For the busy ones, I turned off all the alerts. I only kept notifications for direct messages and when someone actually mentions me. That way, I still see the important stuff. I don't miss anything urgent.

I'm so glad I did it. My afternoon got a lot quieter, and I could finally think. I set my status to focus mode too, so people know I'm heads-down on something. It's funny, but I feel less stressed now. A quiet screen really does help me work better.

If your chat apps are noisy too, I'd say just spend ten minutes on the settings. It feels great. Honestly, I should've done it months ago.
