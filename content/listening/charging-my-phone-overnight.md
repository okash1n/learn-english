Every night before bed, I plug in my phone to charge it. It's a small habit, but it really matters to me. If I forget, I wake up to a dead battery. Then my whole morning feels rushed and stressful. So now I keep the charger right next to my pillow.

For a long time, I left the phone charging until morning. I thought that was normal, and everybody did it. But then a friend told me it isn't great for the battery. Charging all night can slowly wear it down. I wasn't sure if that was really true, so I looked it up.

It turns out modern phones are pretty smart about this. They stop pulling power once they're full. So leaving it overnight isn't as bad as I feared. Still, I decided to change my habit a little. Now I try to unplug it when I wake up early.

There's one more thing I've started doing lately. I put my phone across the room instead of by my bed. That way I can't check it the second I open my eyes. Plus, when the alarm goes off, I have to stand up. It's a simple trick, but it really helps me get moving.

Honestly, this tiny routine has made my mornings calmer. My phone's always ready, and I'm not glued to the screen in bed. It's funny how such a small change can matter. Sometimes the little habits are the ones that stick.
