So I work from home most days, and I have to use the company VPN. It's this little app that connects my laptop to our office network. Without it, I can't reach half our servers. It's fine when it works. But lately it's been driving me a bit crazy.

The problem is, it keeps dropping. I'll be in the middle of pushing my code, and suddenly it just disconnects. Then everything freezes for a second. I get this little red icon, and I have to click reconnect and wait. Don't you hate it when that happens right before a meeting?

At first I thought it was my Wi-Fi. So I moved my router, I restarted my laptop, I tried everything. But the VPN still dropped every twenty minutes or so. It was really annoying, because I couldn't tell what the real cause was.

So yesterday I finally asked our IT team on Slack. And it turns out it wasn't me at all. They said the VPN server was overloaded, because too many people were working remotely that week. I'm actually kind of relieved. It's nice to know I wasn't doing something wrong.

They're adding a second server this weekend, so it should get better soon. For now, I just keep my work saved often, just in case. I'm glad I asked, though. Sometimes you spend hours blaming your own setup, when the real problem is somewhere else.
