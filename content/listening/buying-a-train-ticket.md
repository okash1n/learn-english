title: "Buying a Train Ticket"
title_ja: "電車の切符を買う"

---

Last Saturday I went to the city to see my old friend. I don't take the train very often, so I felt a little nervous. When I got to the station, I found a row of ticket machines near the gate. Some people were already using them, so I waited for my turn.

When it was my turn, I looked at the screen. There were many buttons and lots of small words. First I chose my language, and then the machine showed me a map. I looked for the name of my station, but I couldn't find it right away. My heart started to beat fast.

A kind man behind me saw my worried face. He smiled and asked where I wanted to go. I told him the name, and he pointed to the right button on the map. The price was three hundred yen, so I put in my coins. The machine made a soft sound and gave me my ticket.

I said thank you to the man, and he wished me a good trip. I walked to the gate and put my ticket in. The gate opened, and I felt happy and proud. Buying a ticket was easier than I thought. Next time I won't feel so nervous.

On the train I looked out of the window and smiled. Small problems can feel big when you're alone. But most people are kind and want to help. That day I learned to just ask when I'm not sure. It made my whole trip much better.
