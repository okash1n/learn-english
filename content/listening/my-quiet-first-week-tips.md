When I started my new job, my first week felt very quiet. Everyone was busy, and I didn't want to bother them. So I sat at my desk and tried to look useful. But being quiet all day isn't a good plan. I learned that fast, and I want to share a few simple tips.

My first tip is to write things down. On the first day, people tell you so much. They share names, tools, and passwords. You can't remember all of it. So I kept a small notebook near my keyboard. When someone explained something, I wrote a short note. Later, I read my notes again, and it really helped.

My second tip is to ask small questions early. At first, I was scared to ask. I thought my boss would be annoyed. But that wasn't true. She told me she'd rather answer a quick question than fix a big mistake later. So now I ask when I'm not sure, and I feel much calmer.

My third tip is to say hello to people. I know it sounds simple, but it works. I said hi to the person next to me, and we talked about coffee. The next day, he showed me where the good cups were. Small talk like that makes the office feel warm.

So don't stay too quiet in your first week. Write notes, ask questions, and say hello. You don't need to be perfect. You just need to try a little each day. That's how I got comfortable, and I think you can too.
