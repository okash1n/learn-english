So I finally got my pull request approved this morning. I'm really glad, because it took almost a week. It was a small feature, but the review just kept going and going.

The first round of comments was pretty rough. My reviewer found a few things I'd missed. There was a bug in the way I handled empty input. I felt a bit embarrassed, but honestly, she was right. It's better to catch that stuff early than after it ships.

Then I made the changes and pushed again. I hate it when you fix one thing and something else breaks. That's exactly what happened. One of the tests started failing, so I had to dig into it. Turns out I'd changed a variable name and forgot to update it in one place. Classic mistake.

After that, things went smoothly. I added a couple of comments to explain my logic, and I wrote a clearer commit message. My reviewer left a little thumbs-up emoji and just said, looks good, merging it. That tiny message made my whole day, honestly.

I'm learning that code review isn't about being perfect. It's more like a conversation with your teammates. You share ideas, you catch each other's mistakes, and the code gets better. So now I don't feel nervous when someone reviews my work. Well, maybe just a little.
