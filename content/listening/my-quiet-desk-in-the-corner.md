I sit at a small desk in the corner of the office. It's near the window, so I get a lot of light. At first I didn't like it, because it's far from my team. But now I really enjoy the quiet. I can think clearly and finish my work faster.

In the morning, I get to work early. I make some coffee and check my email. Most days I have a few messages from my boss. She tells me what jobs are most important. So I write a short list and start with the top one. That way, I don't forget anything.

Around noon, my coworkers come by my desk. We talk about our plans for lunch. Sometimes we go to a small shop near the station. The food there is cheap and warm, and the staff are kind. I like these short breaks. They help me feel fresh again.

In the afternoon, the office gets busy and loud. People talk on the phone and walk around a lot. When I need to focus, I put on my headphones. The music is soft, and it helps me block out the noise. My corner is the best place for hard work.

Before I go home, I clean up my desk. I close all my files and turn off my computer. Then I say goodbye to my team and head to the door. I'm happy with my quiet spot now. It's small, but it's really mine.
