Some days my calendar is just full of meetings. This morning I had four in a row, with no real break between them. I'm not complaining, but it's hard to think clearly when you're always jumping from one topic to the next.

The first one was a quick check-in with my team. We share what we did yesterday and what we're stuck on. It's short, so that one's fine. But right after, I had a call with a client who wanted a lot of details. I didn't have time to look at my notes first.

By the third meeting, I was starting to lose focus. Someone asked me a question, and honestly, my mind went blank for a second. I had to say, sorry, can you repeat that? That happens more than I'd like to admit.

So I've started to protect a bit of time for myself. I block thirty minutes in the afternoon and mark it as busy. During that time, I don't take calls. I just answer emails, drink some coffee, and get my head straight. It really makes a difference.

I also try to end my meetings five minutes early now. That gives everyone a small break before the next one. My coworkers seem to like it, and I feel less tired at the end of the day. A few small changes, and work feels a lot calmer.
