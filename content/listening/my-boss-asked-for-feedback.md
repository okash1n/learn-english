Last week my boss called me into a small meeting. I was a little worried at first. I thought maybe I did something wrong. But she just wanted to hear my ideas about our team. She said good feedback from workers helps the whole company. That surprised me, because I'm still new here.

At first, I didn't know what to say. I don't like to complain about my job. But she told me it was safe to be honest. So I talked about our morning meetings. They start too early, and people look tired. I said maybe we could start them a bit later. She wrote my idea down and thanked me.

Then she asked about our email system. It's slow, and it's hard to find old messages. I told her that many of us waste time on it. She agreed and said she'd heard the same thing before. It felt good to know I wasn't the only one.

After the meeting, I felt more relaxed. I learned that speaking up isn't a bad thing. My boss really wants to make our work better. And she can't do that alone. She needs to know what we think every day.

Now I try to share small ideas more often. I don't wait for a big meeting anymore. If something is hard, I just say it kindly. Most of the time, my team listens. That's a nice feeling, and it makes me want to help more.
