Some evenings I get home and I'm really tired. I don't want to cook at all. My feet hurt and my brain feels empty. On days like that, I still have to eat something. So I've learned a few tricks to make dinner easy.

First, I keep simple food in my kitchen. I always have eggs, rice, and some frozen vegetables. If I have those three things, I can make a quick meal in ten minutes. I don't need a special recipe. I just fry an egg and heat some rice.

I try not to make a big mess. When I'm tired, washing lots of dishes feels awful. So I use one pan and one bowl. That way, cleaning up takes only a minute or two. It's a small thing, but it really helps.

Sometimes I still can't find the energy to cook. When that happens, I don't feel bad about it. I eat some bread and fruit, or I buy something on the way home. Food doesn't have to be perfect. It just has to keep me going until tomorrow.

After I eat, I feel a little better. My body relaxes and my mood lifts. So even a simple dinner is worth the effort. I remind myself that taking care of my body matters, even on the hard days.
