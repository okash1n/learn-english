Last week I joined my first online meeting with the whole team. I was a little nervous. I've been at this company for only a month. So I logged in early, about ten minutes before it started. I wanted to check my camera and my microphone first.

At first, my microphone wasn't working. I could hear everyone, but they couldn't hear me. It's a common problem, so I stayed calm. I clicked around the settings and found the right button. After that, my voice came through fine. My boss smiled and said good morning.

The meeting was about our plans for next month. Each person shared what they were working on. When it was my turn, I talked about a small task I'd just finished. My hands were a bit shaky, but I spoke slowly. People nodded, and one coworker said thanks. That felt really good.

I also learned some useful phrases during the call. When I didn't understand something, I asked them to repeat it. Nobody minded at all. They were happy to explain again. So I wrote the new words in my notebook. I want to remember them for next time.

By the end, I felt much more relaxed. Online meetings aren't so scary after all. The key is to prepare early and ask questions. I'm still learning, but I'm getting better each week. Next time, I think I'll say even more.
