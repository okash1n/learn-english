Last week, my manager asked me for a favor. The company was building a new page on our website. It's the page that shows our whole team. So everyone needed a nice photo of their face. I said yes, but honestly I felt a bit nervous about it.

The thing is, I don't really like photos of myself. When someone points a camera at me, I freeze. My smile looks strange, and my shoulders go up. So I kept putting it off. Every day I told myself, I'll do it tomorrow. But tomorrow kept coming, and the photo never happened.

On Friday, a coworker noticed I was the last one left. She's really kind, so she offered to help. We found a quiet corner near the window with good light. She told me to take a slow breath and think about something funny. Then she counted down and took a few shots on her phone.

At first I didn't want to look. But when I finally checked the screen, I was surprised. The photo actually looked like me on a good day. It wasn't perfect, but it was warm and natural. I sent it to my manager right away, before I could change my mind.

Now our team page is live, and my face is up there with everyone else. It feels a little odd to see myself online. But it also feels good to be part of the group. Sometimes a small task feels huge in your head. And then a friend helps, and it turns out fine.
