So yesterday I finished a small feature at work. It wasn't a big change, maybe twenty lines of code. I felt happy and thought it was done. But before we ship anything, a teammate has to check it first. We call that a code review, and I'm still getting used to it.

I sent my code to my coworker, Ken. Then I waited. And I waited some more. It's kind of annoying when you're ready to move on, but you can't. I kept opening the page to see if he'd left a comment yet. Nothing for almost two hours.

When Ken finally replied, he had lots of questions. He asked why I named one thing so strangely. Honestly, he was right, and I felt a little shy about it. He also found a small mistake that I didn't see at all. I'm really glad he caught it before our users did.

So I fixed everything and sent it back to him. This time he answered faster, and he wrote a nice message. He said the code looked clean now, and I could ship it. That felt great after all that waiting. I even said thanks, because his notes really helped me.

Now I get why we do reviews, even when they're slow. Nobody writes perfect code alone, and that's totally fine. A second pair of eyes catches things you just can't see. Next time I'll try to explain my code better from the start. Maybe then the review won't take so long.
