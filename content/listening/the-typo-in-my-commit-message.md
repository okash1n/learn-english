So this morning I pushed some code, and then I saw it. There was a typo right in my commit message. I wrote "fix bugg" with two g's. It's a small thing, but everyone on the team can see it now. Don't you hate it when that happens?

At first I thought, well, it's just a message, who cares. But then a coworker sent me a laughing face. I laughed too, but I still wanted to fix it. I'm the kind of person who likes things clean and tidy.

So I asked my teammate how to change it. She told me I could fix the last message with one simple command. It's not too hard, but you have to be careful. If other people already pulled my code, changing it can cause problems. So I checked first, and lucky me, nobody had.

I ran the command, wrote the message again, and this time I read it twice. Then I pushed it up. It felt so good to see the clean message there. Such a small win, but I was really happy about it.

Now I always read my commit messages before I hit enter. It only takes two seconds, and it saves me from that little embarrassed feeling. Funny how a tiny typo can teach you a habit, right?
