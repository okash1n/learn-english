Every morning I use a card to ride the train. It's really handy. I just tap it at the gate, and the gate opens. I don't need paper tickets anymore. But there's one problem. Sometimes the card runs out of money without warning.

That happened to me last week. I was in a hurry, and I tapped my card at the gate. But the gate stayed shut, and it made a loud beep. People behind me had to stop too. I felt a little embarrassed, so I moved to the side quickly.

Luckily, there's a machine near the gate. You can add money to your card there. I put my card on the tray, and I chose the amount. I usually add about three thousand yen. Then I paid with cash, and the machine gave me a small receipt. The whole thing took less than a minute.

Now I try to check my balance more often. When the number gets low, I add money before I leave home. That way I don't get stuck at a busy gate again. It's a small habit, but it saves me a lot of stress in the morning.

So my advice is simple. Don't wait until your card is empty. A quick top-up the night before makes your morning much smoother. Trust me, your future self will thank you.
