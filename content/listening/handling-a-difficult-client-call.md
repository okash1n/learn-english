Last week I got a call I really didn't want. It was from one of our biggest clients. They weren't happy at all. Our team had promised a report by Monday, but it slipped to Wednesday. The client felt we'd let them down, and honestly, they had a point.

I could hear the frustration in her voice right away. My first instinct was to explain everything. But I've learned that jumping straight to excuses just makes things worse. So I stayed quiet and let her finish. I said I understood why she was upset, and I meant it. Sometimes people just need to feel heard first.

Once she'd calmed down a little, I told her exactly what happened. One of our developers was out sick, and we didn't plan for it well. I didn't blame anyone. I just owned the mistake on behalf of the team. Then I focused on the fix. I promised her the full report by the next morning, and I offered a short call to walk her through it.

She still wasn't thrilled, but her tone softened. What really mattered to her was knowing we were taking it seriously. Before we hung up, she actually thanked me for being straight with her. That surprised me, in a good way.

Looking back, that call taught me something useful. Clients don't expect us to be perfect. They just want honesty and a clear next step. When something goes wrong, hiding it never helps. Owning it, staying calm, and fixing it fast, that's what builds trust over time.
