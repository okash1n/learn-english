Last week my manager asked me to plan a team lunch. At first I felt a little nervous, because I've never done this before. But she said it was a good chance to help everyone relax. So I said yes, and I started thinking about what to do.

First, I asked my team where they wanted to eat. Some people like fish, and some people don't eat meat. So I made a short list of places near our office. Then I sent a quick message to everyone. I asked them to pick their favorite spot by Friday.

Most people chose the small Italian place down the street. It's quiet, and the food isn't too expensive. I called them on Thursday and booked a table for eight. The woman on the phone was very kind, and she saved a nice spot by the window for us.

On the day of the lunch, everyone came on time. We talked about our work, but we also talked about our weekends and our families. My manager smiled a lot, and she thanked me in front of the team. That made me feel really happy and proud.

I learned something important that day. Small things, like a shared meal, can bring people closer. Now our team talks more easily at work. So next time, I won't feel nervous about planning something like this. I'll just enjoy it.
